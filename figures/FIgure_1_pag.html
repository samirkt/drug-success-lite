Figure 1. Defining the drug-indication approval-forecast task. (A) Comparison of prediction units across related task formulations. This work uniquely targets drug–indication pairs, enabling individualized disease-specific approval scoring without requiring trial-level features. Shaded row indicates this work. (B) Illustrative case studies showing indication-level discrimination. The same molecule receives distinct predicted approval probabilities (p) across diseases, directionally consistent with known approval labels. Filled squares, approved indication; open squares, not approved. Scores are model outputs on the held-out test set. (C) Locked cohort summary. Training set: 14,134 drug–indication pairs (2,469 approved, 11,665 failed; ~1:5 ratio, shown in bar). Held-out post-2019 test set: 2,637 pairs including 624 approvals. Temporal split is designed to mimic prospective deployment conditions.
