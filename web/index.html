Drug approval probability tool

About

Predict whether a drug candidate will reach FDA approval

Enter a drug and disease. The tool estimates the probability of reaching FDA approval for that specific pair, plus a per-trial phase-transition estimate, scored by trained XGBoost models on features resolved on demand from public databases.

Drug and disease

Paste a drug name, ID, or SMILES string. Everything else fills in automatically.

Resolves through RxNorm → DrugBank → ChEMBL → canonical SMILES → MolFormer embedding. Biologics skip the structural step and use target identity directly.

Auto-maps to MeSH descriptor, then cross-walks to EFO and MONDO for Open Targets queries.

Classified as large pharma / biotech / academic / government via SEC EDGAR. Prior approvals counted before IND year only.

Freezes every database feature (Open Targets, ChEMBL, Reactome) to the version that existed in this year. No future information leaks into the prediction.

This prototype uses curated demonstration data. Production output will reflect actual XGBoost model output for the queried candidate, with isotonic calibration and SHAP attribution computed on demand.

Prototype for the calibrated drug-disease FDA approval prediction framework described in Tagkopoulos et al. (in preparation). Demo candidates use representative numbers and historical analogs; production output requires the trained XGBoost model loaded server-side. Pipeline source, training data, and model artifacts will be released open-source under the project repository.
